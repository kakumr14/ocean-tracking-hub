📖 Freight Glossary

Common logistics and freight forwarding terms explained.

BL (Bill of Lading)

Definition:
Document issued by carrier acknowledging receipt of cargo.

Example:
Shipment from Shanghai to Nhava Sheva under BL ABC123456.

Usage:
Used for cargo release, customs clearance and shipment tracking.

MBL (Master Bill of Lading)

Definition:
Bill of Lading issued by shipping line.

Example:
MSC issues MBL for ocean shipment.

Usage:
Used between carrier and freight forwarder.

HBL (House Bill of Lading)

Definition:
Bill of Lading issued by freight forwarder.

Example:
Forwarder issues HBL to shipper.

Usage:
Used between forwarder and customer.

ETA

Definition:
Estimated Time of Arrival.

Example:
ETA Nhava Sheva: 25 June.

Usage:
Used for delivery planning and customs activities.

ETD

Definition:
Estimated Time of Departure.

Example:
ETD Shanghai: 10 June.

Usage:
Used for shipment planning.

VGM

Definition:
Verified Gross Mass of packed container.

Example:
Container VGM = 21,500 KG.

Usage:
Mandatory before vessel loading.

FCL

Definition:
Full Container Load shipment.

Example:
One exporter books complete 40HC.

Usage:
Suitable for large cargo volumes.

LCL

Definition:
Less than Container Load shipment.

Example:
5 CBM cargo consolidated with others.

Usage:
Suitable for small shipments.

CY (Container Yard)

Definition:
Area where containers are stored in port.

Example:
Container delivered to CY before cutoff.

Usage:
Used in export and import container handling.

CFS (Container Freight Station)

Definition:
Facility for consolidation and deconsolidation of cargo.

Example:
LCL cargo moved through CFS.

Usage:
Common in LCL shipments.
